Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a hearty Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy ricotta or béchamel, and melted mozzarella cheese. The layers are baked until bubbly and golden, creating a perfect combination of textures.

The pasta softens as it absorbs the sauce, while the cheeses provide a creamy, indulgent finish. With every bite, you get a balanced mix of savory flavors and satisfying comfort. This dish is perfect for family dinners or gatherings, as it's both filling and crowd-pleasing.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (24 oz) jar of marinara sauce
  • 1 (15 oz) container of ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for cooking

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef, onion, and garlic. Cook until the beef is browned and the onion is soft. Drain excess fat.
  4. Stir in the marinara sauce, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. In a medium bowl, mix together the ricotta cheese, egg, and half of the Parmesan cheese.
  6. In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
  7. Place 3 to 4 lasagna noodles on top of the sauce.
  8. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by a layer of mozzarella cheese.
  9. Repeat the layers, ending with a final layer of meat sauce and the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses.
  10. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  11. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 to 20 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  12. Let the lasagna cool for 10 minutes before serving.
